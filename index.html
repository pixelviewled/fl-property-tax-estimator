Search by address or parcel number, enter what you'd pay, and see the estimated taxes for you as the new owner. Ad valorem is recalculated after the sale; non-ad valorem is pulled itemized from the actual Polk County Tax Collector bill.

    How the numbers are sourced. Address search uses the Polk County Property Appraiser GIS (occasionally offline — if so, enter your 18-digit parcel number to go straight to the bill). The current tax breakdown — itemized non-ad valorem (CDD, fire, solid waste, stormwater), millage, exemptions and totals — is read live from the official Polk County Tax Collector bill (polk.floridatax.us). Ad valorem for a new owner is recalculated by removing the Save Our Homes cap (assessed value resets to market on sale) and applying the parcel's actual millage to your purchase price; the displayed full ad valorem is before any early-payment discount. Non-ad valorem is carried forward as-is. This is an estimate; the Property Appraiser and Tax Collector set official figures.
